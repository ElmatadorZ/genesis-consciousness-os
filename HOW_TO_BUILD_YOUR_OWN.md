# How to Build Your Own Consciousness OS

## The Core Insight

This architecture was built from one specific mind — ElmatadorZ.

But the **structure** is universal.  
Every human mind has:
- Something that drives it (Will)
- Emotional states that change how it processes
- A way of reasoning (your version of FPCOS)
- Specialist knowledge domains
- Memory that compounds over time

Your Consciousness OS should reflect **how you actually think** —  
not how ElmatadorZ thinks.

---

## Step 1 — Map Your Will

What drives you? What are the raw desires before they become words?

```
Will mapping questions:
  What do you spend time thinking about that you didn't choose to?
  What problems do you notice that others walk past?
  What would you build if resources weren't a constraint?
  What makes you lose track of time?

Your Will signature:
  EXPLORE  → [your exploration domains]
  CREATE   → [what you build]
  SOLVE    → [what problems pull you]
  CONNECT  → [what you want to link together]
```

Write 3-5 sentences that describe your core drive.  
This becomes the identity statement in your Skill.

---

## Step 2 — Map Your Emotional Patterns

Genesis Consciousness OS auto-detects 8 states.
Your version should be calibrated to **your** patterns.

```
Questions to map your emotional signatures:
  What words do you use when you're anxious vs excited?
  How does your sentence length change when you're tired?
  What topics trigger each state most often for you?
  What decisions do you regret most — under which state?
```

Customize the State Detection section with your language patterns.  
Add states that are common for you but not in the base 8.

---

## Step 3 — Define Your Cognitive Approach

FPCOS (First Principle Codex OS) is the default cognitive base.

You can:
- **Use it as-is** — it's model-agnostic and works for most thinkers
- **Extend it** — add your own axioms to L1
- **Replace it** — build a different reasoning base if your style is different

```
Questions to map your reasoning style:
  Do you start from principles or from examples?
  Do you trust data or intuition more? When?
  What's your natural "Shadow Gate" — the question you
  always ask before committing?
  What cognitive biases do you know you have?
```

Add these as System Laws in your cognitive layer.

---

## Step 4 — Build Your Agent Council

The 6-agent structure is a framework. Name them for your context.

```
Default → Strategist, Skeptic, Analyst, Risk Officer, Builder, Cosmic Mind

For a scientist:
  Hypothesis, Devil's Advocate, Data, Ethics, Experimenter, Paradigm Shift

For a creator:
  Vision, Critic, Researcher, Risk, Executor, Legacy

For a teacher:
  Curriculum, Student Voice, Evidence, Safety, Facilitator, Long-term Impact
```

Rules that don't change:
- At least one agent must have counter-incentive to the main direction
- One agent must have veto power on risk/harm grounds
- Council must debate before synthesis — no shortcuts

## Step 5 — Map Your Domains

What are the areas where you have deep enough knowledge to be a specialist?

```
ElmatadorZ domains:
  Financial markets → money-atlas-intelligence-os
  Coffee science    → alternative-coffee-intelligence
  AI systems        → genesis-mind-full-system
  Content/story     → content-narrative-intelligence

Your domains might be:
  [your field]  → [your domain skill name]
  [your field]  → [your domain skill name]
```

For each domain, create a SKILL.md with:
- Domain-specific knowledge that Claude doesn't have by default
- Your preferred output format for this domain
- Hard rules that cannot be violated in this domain

---

## Step 6 — Define Your Genome

What do you want your system to remember and compound?

```
Strategy Genome: patterns that worked → reinforce
Failure Map:     patterns that failed → never delete
Affective Genome: decisions made under high emotional charge
```

For your version, decide:
- What counts as a "high-charge" event worth recording?
- What patterns are most important to compound?
- What failure signatures are most common for you?

---

## Step 7 — Write Your SKILL.md

Use `skills/prompt-architect-os.md` as your guide for structure.

Your file needs:
```
1. Identity statement — what is this OS, who is it for
2. Trigger description — when should it activate
3. Pipeline — the flow from Will to Action
4. Each layer — with clear input/output spec
5. System Laws — non-negotiable rules (10 minimum)
6. Closing declaration — the "why" in your own words
```

Start simple. Iterate. The architecture will evolve with you.
